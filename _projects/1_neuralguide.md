A wearable earpiece device with camera and IMU sensor powered by our TinyLLM hardware platform performs fully on-device, multimodal inference without requiring internet connectivity.

## Overview

Equipped with an integrated camera, the device runs a visual instruction model (LlaVa-Onevision) to assist visually impaired individuals or elderly users in locating objects or navigating environments, such as identifying road signs or nearby landmarks.

## Key Features

- **Fully On-device Processing**: No internet connectivity required for inference
- **Multimodal Input**: Combines camera and IMU sensor data
- **Real-time Assistance**: Natural language interaction for visual and navigation tasks
- **Software-Hardware Co-design**: Optimized for power efficiency and performance

## Current Challenges

- Enhancing the device's positioning and reasoning capabilities
- Improving accuracy and reliability in object localization
- Addressing limitations in context understanding
