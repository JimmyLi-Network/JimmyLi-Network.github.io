I am a PhD student in Computer Science at the [University of Wisconsin-Madison](https://wisc.edu), advised by [Professor Suman Banerjee](https://pages.cs.wisc.edu/~suman/).

My research interests span **networked and wireless systems**, **efficient on-device inference for large language models (LLMs)**, and **mobile & wearable devices**. With extensive experience in hardware design and wireless systems, I explore building systems through a software-hardware co-design approach.

## Research Interests

- **Networked/Wireless Systems**: Wireless sensing systems for human vital signs, mmWave integrated sensing and communication (ISAC), Ultra-Wide Band (UWB) sensing systems. Extensive experience in RF, microwave design, and PCB design.

- **Efficient LLM On-device Inference**: Developing efficient LLM inference for small, battery-powered edge devices using software-hardware co-design approaches with customized C++ frameworks, enabling deployment of large multi-modal models (Llama-3, LLaVa, Qwen) with various quantization methods (GPTQ, AWQ, 1-bit).

- **Multimodal LLMs for Wearables**: Exploring multi-modalities (sensor data, camera) for mobile and wearable devices, enabling real-time, on-device assistance for visual and language tasks.

## Experience

I spent one year at [NEC Laboratories America](https://www.nec-labs.com/) as a Research Intern (2020-2021), where I developed a scalable, high-resolution 16x16 Ultra-Wideband (UWB) radar system for monitoring human vital signs. This system employed a hardware-algorithm co-design with a multi-head attention contrastive learning model to accurately extract vital signs from multiple moving targets in complex indoor environments.
